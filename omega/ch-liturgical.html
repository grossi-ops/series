Why 360° = 2π and Why the Sabbath

The Chaldean astronomers (Babylonian mathematical astronomy, c. 700–400 BCE) divided the circle into 360°, the number closest to the solar year (365.25 days) that is divisible by many small integers. The Chaldean calendar grouped days into weeks of 7, with the 7th day as the rest day — a social analog of the periodic return built into the contact structure.
