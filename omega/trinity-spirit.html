What Two Systems Can Actually Share

Genesis (the Father) is an unfolding with no specified shape. Union (the Son) is two such unfoldings joined into one, their stability bounded by whichever was less stable — unless the two are orthogonal, in which case the union loses nothing. What remains unanswered is the most practical question of all: when two systems meet, what, exactly, do they exchange? Not everything. Only what they already have in common.

Definition · Resonance / Koinonia (R-operator)
R𝛺[ψ] = Σ{n : |ωₙ − ω| < δ} ⟨uₙ|ψ⟩ uₙ

A system ψ is decomposed into its eigenmodes uₙ, each with its own eigenfrequency ωₙ. The resonance operator keeps only the modes whose frequency falls within δ of a target ω — and discards the rest. Two systems "communicate" exactly along the modes that survive this projection in both directions at once.

This is what koinonia — the New Testament's word for the Spirit-given fellowship of the early church — names mathematically. It is not merger: ψ is not replaced by some average of itself and another system. It is selection: out of everything ψ could in principle resonate with, only the shared band actually couples. "Where two or three are gathered" is not a statement about head-count. It is a statement about which frequencies are present in the room.

The Last Book, Because Infinity Is Bounded

The final book of the Christian canon calls itself the Apocalypse — the unveiling — and names its subject the Alpha and the Omega, "the first and the last" (Rev 22:13). Read only as a claim about endings, that can sound like closure for its own sake. Read through Theorem 5.2, it is a more specific and more interesting claim: that an infinite process has an exact limit.

π→ φ→ μ→ η→ Δ→ Σ→ Ω → τ = 2
Theorem 5.2 · Convergence to the Omega Point
π → φ → μ → η → Δ → Σ → Ω    lim Ω = τ = 2

Each step of the recurrence ladder is an n-bonacci constant — the unique positive root of xⁿ = xⁿ⁻¹ + ... + 1. There is one for every n; the sequence is, in principle, endless. But it is monotonically increasing and bounded above by 2. It never reaches 2 at any finite step — and yet it has nowhere else to go. The limit is exact.

That is what "the last book" names, mathematically: not the end of a story, but the fact that an unbounded sequence has an exact, attainable bound. The Father's unfolding has no specified endpoint. The Spirit's resonance is what lets that unfolding find, among infinitely many possible frequencies, the finitely many it can actually share with another. The Omega Point is the statement that the whole pattern — unfolding, union, resonance, repeated without limit — converges anyway. Infinity, here, is bounded.
