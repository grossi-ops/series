Two Vocabularies, One Operator

"In the beginning was the Word" — and "the Word became flesh." Theology has always claimed that becoming converges on something. Omega Point shows the exact operator that convergence is, and gives it the name a contemplative tradition would recognize.

Omega Point is a companion to the Principia Orthogona series — the seven-volume dm³ framework, mechanised in Lean 4 with zero sorrys in its core chain. Its most general formulation, Generative Contact Mechanics (GCM), organizes a system's behavior into four operators — expansion, coherence, resonance, and unification — and the same recurrence-ladder constants (τ = 2, φ ≈ 1.618, ε₀ = 1/3, the g-series) govern both series. What changes is the language used to describe what they mean — semigroups and Lie brackets on one side, Genesis and Logos on the other.

This is not a metaphor bolted onto unrelated mathematics. It is the same operators, the same theorems, the same convergence — read by a reader for whom "in the beginning," "the Word," "where two or three are gathered," and "the two become one" are not unfamiliar phrases, and who wants to know whether there is something rigorous underneath them. There is.

Each rung is the limit of an n-bonacci recurrence — Fibonacci, Tribonacci, Tetranacci, Pentanacci, Hexabonacci — and every one of them, as n grows, approaches τ = 2 a little more closely without ever reaching or exceeding it. Teilhard de Chardin called this limit the Omega Point. The Lean 4 proof in GTCT calls it τ = 2. Omega Point, the book, is about why both names point at the same place.

The Ancient Transmission

The Sumerians said they were given writing by beings who arrived before them. Four thousand years later, we read their tablets. That is the gift: not an answer, but the tool that lets a question outlive the asker.

The mathematical structures in this book — the recurrence ladder, the period T* = 2π, the stability threshold ε₀ = 1/3 — did not begin with modern mathematics. They appear, encoded in different vocabularies, across four millennia of human knowing. The Chaldean base-60 arithmetic is still alive in our hours, our minutes, our degrees. The Kabbalistic Tree of Life is a ladder of ascent with exactly the same convergent structure as the n-bonacci sequence. The Annunaki story and the film Arrival are the same plot: a non-human intelligence arrives and gives not an answer but a language — and the language changes your relationship to time.

The chapter Abraham of Ur traces this arc from cuneiform to Kabbalah to Chain.lean. The transmission is unbroken. The tablet is still speaking.
