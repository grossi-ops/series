This map tracks where each thread lives, which threads converge at each node, and what to do with overlaps — safe reinforcement vs. elevation opportunity vs. content to drop.

D2 Writing Strategy

What to drop from C1 scaffolding: The "Quantum Weave" framing using "quantum" loosely (Mini-Beast §4 seed text, pp. 48–51) should not be carried verbatim into D2. The Daśāvatāra-as-metaphor framing (avatar = adaptive stage, C1 convenience) should be replaced by the operator-sequence reading. These two items wear the right ideas in the wrong clothes.

What to elevate: The Daśāvatāra-as-operator-sequence (C→K→F→U→G maps to avatars I–X structurally, with Paraśurāma = K = threshold VI) needs its Lean grounding made explicit — Threshold.lean is the formal instance. The Metzinger no-self discussion in D2 §4 connects directly to IterationGap.lean's CollatzLyapunov as the formal limit of self-reference: mention this. The Quantum Weave upgrade is: replace the loose "quantum" with the formal PhaseVector from Crystal/G6.lean — 12 dimensions, D₆ symmetry, and the crystal_lockin sorry as an honest marker of what remains open in the formal theory.

Safe overlaps: The 112 Dhāraṇās as phenomenological protocol, the Cajueiro nut as resonant-chamber instantiation of the fixed point, the Seven Disciples transmission model, and the Varela/Maturana bridge all appear in multiple layers without contradicting each other. Reference but don't re-explain.

The core IKIGAI: x* · Collatz · lemniscate · K-crossing · sorry roadmap · Kālabhairava all have all four layers present. These are the load-bearing nodes. The ch-d2-parashurama.html full text should ensure §2 (K as formal hinge) cites Threshold.lean explicitly, §3 (Tantrāloka as field theory) grounds in Crystal/G6.lean PhaseVector, and §5 (transmission) closes by pointing at IterationGap.lean as the formal analogue of proof-obligation handoff from guru to disciple.
