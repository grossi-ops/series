Do I need to know mathematics?
No. The mathematical framework is the scaffold for the writing — you learn it by reading and annotating, not by taking exams. The AXLE section (Lean 4) is clearly marked optional for mathematically inclined students.
What English level do I need?
The course is designed for C1 entry and D2 exit (above CEFR C2 in academic register). If you can read a scientific abstract in English and understand most of it, you are ready.
Will my papers actually be published?
Yes. Zenodo is a CERN-operated open repository that issues permanent DOIs. Your papers will be publicly accessible, permanently archived, and citable. This is how pre-prints work at arXiv, bioRxiv, and SSRN — the same infrastructure used by professional researchers.
Is this self-paced?
Yes. The structure is 16 weeks but there are no live sessions or deadlines. Work at the pace that fits your schedule. The weekly structure is a guide, not a constraint.
