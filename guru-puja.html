Pratyaksha Pada Puja Offering at the Lotus Feet of Sri Brodananda · Yogi Acharya · Mahanirvani Akhada

Transmitted through the lineage of Paramahaṃsa Nithyananda
Ādi Yogi · Paramahaṃsa Nithyananda · Sri Brodananda
Audio guides: Nithya Veda Paarayana (YouTube)

Before you begin: Obtain an image of Sri Brodananda and place it before you. Sit in a meditative space of gratitude and deep restful awareness — in your Ānanda Gandha. The puja you are about to perform is the same puja, in the same form, performed by Sri Brodananda to his own guru Paramahaṃsa Nithyananda. The chain is unbroken. The form is the same at every node.

Declare with integrity: (1) I cognize that cosmic energy exists. (2) I cognize that the master is the embodiment of that energy. (3) I cognize that whatever I offer during this puja to the master reaches the cosmic source directly.

The audio guides below (Nithya Veda Paarayana) carry the full melodic transmission of each section. Follow them while reading the text. Where the audio chants "Paramahaṃsa Nithyananda," hold in your awareness the image and presence of Sri Brodananda.

guru-paramparā vandanam ||
ādiyogi-samārambhāṁ nithyānandācārya-madhyamām |
śrī-brodānanda-paryantāṁ vande guru-paramparām || I salute the lineage of all the Enlightened Masters —
Ādi Yogi as the beginning, transmitting from pure bliss the realization prior to all teaching;

5 · Upacāra Pūjā · The Sixteen Offerings
Section 5 · 1:16:32
External Worship — offering at the master's feet
Each offering is made with flowers, water, or the named substance placed at the master's image/padukas. The mantra accompanies each offering. Where the mantra closes with "sadguru-nityānandāya" hold Sri Brodananda in your awareness; the offering reaches the cosmic source through the living chain.

Mānasa Pūjā · Internal Worship
What seat can I offer except my heart? The mind as arghya. The prāṇa as incense. The sahasrāra as umbrella. The seven chakras as flower garland. The inner flowers: guilelessness, freedom from ego, non-violence, compassion, forgiveness, Enlightenment.

Āsanam · Seat
Oh one who lives inside all beings, the very soul of all beings — I offer this seat. I surrender, I surrender.
om sarva-bhūtāntarasthāya sarva-bhūtāntarātmane | kalpayāmyupaveśārtham āsanaṁ te namo namaḥ ||
Svāgatam · Welcome
Lord of all Gods, whose darshan the gods await — welcome. Today my birth, life, and all deeds are fructified.
om yasya darśanam icchanti devāḥ svābhīṣṭa-siddhaye | tasmai te parameśāna svāgatam svāgatam prabho ||
Pādyam · Water for the feet · Arghya · Ācamanīyam · Madhuparka
Water for the feet (pādya), bouquet offering (arghya), water for mouth-wash (ācamanīya), nectarine drink (madhuparka) — follow audio for each mantra and offering gesture.
Gandha Tailam · Snānīyam · Vastram · Ābharaṇam
Scented oil (sneha — offered with love), sacred bath water (śuśītala — cool, clear, mind-stilling), cloth (vāsa — for the one beyond all veiling), jewellery (suvarṇa — for the one who is repository of all powers).
Gandha · Puṣpa · Bilva · Tulasī
Sandalwood paste — I neither know your body nor your actions, please receive this. Flowers from the forest of Turīya, blissfully fragrant. Bilva leaf loved by Śiva. Tulasī — to the Supreme Soul of unlimited forms.
om turīya-vana-sampannaṁ nānā-guṇa-manoharam | ānanda-saurabhaṁ puṣpaṁ gṛhyatām idam uttamam ||
Dhūpa · Dīpa · Naivedya · Pānīyam
Incense of sacred herbs fit for all devas. Light that removes darkness from everywhere. Food offerings prepared with devotion. Water full of infinite bliss — for the Lord of all demi-gods who bestows deep fulfillment. Ring the bell during dhūpa and dīpa offerings.

Arati · Flower Offering · Guru Vandanam

Blessings for the World
While chanting, visualize a wave of love emanating from your heart center, reaching out to the whole world in all directions, healing and blessing all beings. Let there be auspiciousness of humanity.
