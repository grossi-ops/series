The Seven Axioms

Os Sete Axiomas
Seven irreducible axioms. None is derivable from the others. All are machine-verified in Lean 4 via the AXLE proof environment. The logical dependency chain runs: Axioms → Field → Operators → Correspondence → Orthogonality → Recursion → Emergence.

Axiom 1
Axiom 1 (Contact Structure) / Axioma 1 (Estrutura de Contato) ✓ Lean 4
Contact Structure
There exists a smooth contact manifold $M$ with contact form $\alpha$ such that $d\alpha$ is non-degenerate on $\ker \alpha$.
Existe uma variedade suave de contato $M$ com forma de contato $\alpha$ tal que $d\alpha$ é não-degenerada em $\ker \alpha$.
(M, α) contact manifold, dα|_{ker α} non-degenerate ✓(Lean 4)

The contact manifold is the geometric stage on which all GTCT dynamics unfold. The non-degeneracy of $d\alpha$ on $\ker\alpha$ is precisely what distinguishes contact geometry from foliation theory: the kernel of $\alpha$ cannot be integrated to a surface. This is the irreducibility that forces the dynamics to keep moving.

In the dm³ toy model, $M = \mathbb{R}^2_+ \times \mathbb{R}$ with cylindrical coordinates $(r,\theta,z)$ and $\alpha = dz - r^2 d\theta$. The Reeb vector field satisfies $\iota_R d\alpha = 0$ and $\alpha(R) = 1$, giving the flow $\dot\theta = 1$, $\dot r = \dot z = 0$ — pure angular motion at constant radius and height.

Axiom 2
Axiom 2 (Generative Operator Chain) / Axioma 2 (Cadeia de Operadores Generativos) ✓ Lean 4
G = U ∘ F ∘ K ∘ C
There exists $G = U \circ F \circ K \circ C : M \to M$. $C$ is injective and contractive; $K$ decreases potential $\Phi$; $F$ is non-injective with finite branch set; $U$ decreases $\Phi$ and drives orbits toward a fixed point.
Existe $G = U \circ F \circ K \circ C : M \to M$. $C$ é injetora e contrativa; $K$ decresce $\Phi$; $F$ é não-injetora com ramo finito; $U$ decresce $\Phi$ e dirige órbitas a ponto fixo.
G = U ∘ F ∘ K ∘ C : M → M ✓(Lean 4) C: injective + contractive | K: decreases Φ F: non-injective, finite branch set | U: drives to fixed point

The operator chain is non-commutative by construction — this is the geometric origin of time's irreversibility. $C$ (Compress) is the wholesale operator: acquire at the source. $K$ (Threshold) is curvature at the critical point $\kappa^*$. $F$ (Fold) is the Whitney $A_1$ singularity, the commitment point from which there is no return. $U$ (Unfold) is the retail operator: transmit at value.

Non-commutativity: $G' = F \circ U \circ C \circ K$ produces a distinct orbit. The order is not a convention — it is what the topology demands.

Axiom 3
Axiom 3 (Temporal Field) / Axioma 3 (Campo Temporal) ✓ Lean 4
Non-Vanishing Temporal Vector Field
A non-vanishing vector field $T$ on $M$ with flow $\varphi_t$ satisfying $\varphi_t^*\alpha = e^{f(t)}\alpha$ for some smooth $f$.
Campo vetorial $T$ não-nulo em $M$ com fluxo $\varphi_t$ satisfazendo $\varphi_t^* \alpha = e^{f(t)} \alpha$ para alguma $f$ suave.
φ_t* α = e^{f(t)} α, T ≠ 0 ✓(Lean 4)

The temporal field $T$ is not a parameter added to the geometry — it is a geometric object on $M$ with its own dynamics. The conformal reparametrization $\varphi_t^*\alpha = e^{f(t)}\alpha$ means time scales the contact structure without destroying it. This is the fifth operator: $T$ completes the circuit $C \to K \to F \to U \to T \to$ (return).

Axiom 4
Axiom 4 (Triad of Invariants) / Axioma 4 (Tríade de Invariantes) ✓ Lean 4
Three Global Invariants
Three global invariants: $I_1$ (Orthogonality) — consecutive phase vectors are orthogonal in $\mathbb{R}^{12}$; $I_2$ (Nilpotency) — critical exponent $\varepsilon^* = 1/3$; $I_3$ (Spectral Collapse) — eigenvalue spectrum collapses at resonance.
Três invariantes globais: $I_1$ (Ortogonalidade); $I_2$ (Nilpotência) — $\varepsilon^* = 1/3$; $I_3$ (Colapso Espectral).
I₁: ⟨δᵢ, δᵢ₊₁⟩ = 0 (orthogonality of consecutive phases) I₂: ε* = 1/3 (nilpotency / stability radius) I₃: spectral collapse at resonance ✓(Lean 4)

The stability radius $\varepsilon_0 = 1/3$ is the outer basin boundary: within $\|x - x^*\| \leq \varepsilon_0$, the G-orbit contracts to the fixed point. This value derives from $\mu_{\max} = -2$ and $\sup\|\text{Hess}\,V\| = 2$ at the limit cycle: $\varepsilon_0 = |\mu_{\max}|/(2(1 + \sup\|\text{Hess}\,V\|)) = 2/6 = 1/3$. The number $1/3$ is not tunable.

Axiom 5
Axiom 5 (Twelve-Phase Cycle) / Axioma 5 (Ciclo de Doze Fases) ✓ Lean 4
$D_1 \to D_2 \to \cdots \to D_{12} \to D_1$
Dimensional evolution proceeds through 12 phases $D_1, \ldots, D_{12}$ with cyclic indexing $D_{i+12} \equiv D_i$ and period $T^* = 2\pi$.
A evolução dimensional procede por 12 fases $D_1, \ldots, D_{12}$ com $D_{i+12} \equiv D_i$ e período $T^* = 2\pi$.
D₁ → D₂ → ⋯ → D₁₂ → D₁, T* = 2π ✓(Lean 4)

The 12-phase structure arises from the four base operators ($C$, $K$, $F$, $U$) each contributing three dimensional operators (one per invariant: $I_1$, $I_2$, $I_3$). $4 \times 3 = 12$. The period $T^* = 2\pi$ is the Reeb period of the contact structure — it is not chosen, it is the only value compatible with the non-degeneracy of $d\alpha$.

The g-series: $g^0$ (quiescent, 0 cycles), $g^6$ (stable, 6 cycles), $g^{33}$ (threshold, 33 independent orthogonality constraints from $\binom{12}{2} - 33 = 33$), $g^{64}$ (saturation, $64 = 2^6$ — six binary bits index the 64-state operator space).

Axiom 6
Axiom 6 (Phase–Operator Correspondence) / Axioma 6 (Correspondência Fase–Operador) ✓ Lean 4
Bijection $\Phi : \{D_1,\ldots,D_{12}\} \to \{O_1,\ldots,O_{12}\}$
There is a bijection $\Phi : \{D_1,\ldots,D_{12}\} \to \{O_1,\ldots,O_{12}\}$ with $\Phi(D_i) = O_i$.
Bijeção $\Phi : \{D_1,\ldots,D_{12}\} \to \{O_1,\ldots,O_{12}\}$ com $\Phi(D_i) = O_i$.
Φ(D_i) = O_i ✓(Lean 4)

Every phase has exactly one operator and every operator has exactly one phase. The cycle is rigid. This is the existence claim for Theorem 4.1 (Correspondence) — Axiom 6 asserts it; Chapter 4 proves uniqueness, injectivity, and surjectivity.

Axiom 7
Axiom 7 (Recursive Evolution) / Axioma 7 (Evolução Recursiva) ✓ Lean 4
The Circuit is Generative, Not Periodic
$R = O_{12} \circ \cdots \circ O_1$ generates the sequence $x_0, x_{12}, x_{24}, \ldots$ converging to a fixed point. $g_{33} = 33$, $g_{64} = 64 = 2^6$.
$R = O_{12} \circ \cdots \circ O_1$ gera sequência $x_0, x_{12}, \ldots$ convergindo a ponto fixo. $g_{33} = 33$, $g_{64} = 64 = 2^6$.
R = O₁₂ ∘ ⋯ ∘ O₁, x_{12k} → x*, g₃₃ = 33, g₆₄ = 64 ✓(Lean 4) Theorem T1 (Spiral Return): G⁶⁴(x₀) ≠ x₀ — circuit is generative

Theorem T1 (Spiral Return, proved in AXLE): $G^{64}(x_0) \neq x_0$ and $G^{128}(x_0) \neq x_0$. The 64-step orbit does not return to its origin. This is the geometric meaning of generativity: each complete circuit moves the system to a structurally identical but distinct configuration. The seed does not repeat — it transforms.

Remark — Role of g₃₃ and g₆₄

$g_{33} = 33$ counts the independent quadratic constraints on the rank-1 corrections $u_i w_i^T$ arising from the 12-phase cycle: $\binom{12}{2} = 66$ pairs minus 33 sign-symmetric duplicates, leaving 33 independent orthogonality constraints (SH1). $g_{64} = 64 = 2^6$ encodes the binary dimension of the operator-index space (6 bits index 64 states), fixing the ambient representation dimension in the Lean 4 formalization.

Neither constant appears directly in the contraction constant $\kappa$ or the orthogonality proof — those depend only on $\varepsilon^* = 1/3$ and $T^* = 2\pi$. Their detailed role in the higher-dimensional formalization is developed in Volume VI.
