The Dimensional Field

O Campo Dimensional
Introduces $\Delta : M \to \mathbb{R}^{12}$ — twelve phase functions living on one contact manifold. Axioms 8–9 and four structural definitions. The field is the bridge between the abstract operator chain and the concrete 12-phase cycle.
Overview

The dimensional field $\Delta : M \to \mathbb{R}^{12}$ encodes the state of the system across all twelve phases simultaneously. It is not twelve separate functions — it is one map into $\mathbb{R}^{12}$, meaning every point $x \in M$ has a well-defined 12-dimensional phase vector $\Delta(x) = (\delta_1(x), \ldots, \delta_{12}(x))$. The unit-norm condition $|\Delta(x)| = 1$ means the field lives on the unit sphere $S^{11} \subset \mathbb{R}^{12}$.

The temporal compatibility axiom (Axiom 9) says that following the Reeb flow $\varphi_t$ rotates the phase vector by $e^{iT^*t}$ — one full rotation per period $T^* = 2\pi$. This ties the geometry of the contact manifold directly to the algebraic structure of the 12-phase cycle.

Axiom 8 — The Field Exists
Axiom 8 (Existence of the Dimensional Field) ✓ Lean 4
$\Delta : M \to \mathbb{R}^{12}$
There exists a smooth map $\Delta : M \to \mathbb{R}^{12}$ with linearly independent component functions $\delta_1,\ldots,\delta_{12}$ and $|\Delta(x)| = 1$ for all $x \in M$.
Existe $\Delta : M \to \mathbb{R}^{12}$ suave com componentes linearmente independentes e $|\Delta(x)| = 1$.
Δ : M → ℝ¹², δ₁,…,δ₁₂ linearly independent, |Δ(x)| = 1 ∀x ∈ M

Linear independence of the components is essential: it rules out degenerate configurations where two phases collapse into one. The unit-norm condition is a normalisation — it fixes the scale and allows the orthogonality arguments in Chapter 5 to be stated cleanly in terms of inner products on $S^{11}$.

Axiom 9 — Temporal Compatibility
Axiom 9 (Temporal Compatibility) ✓ Lean 4
$\Delta(\varphi_t(x)) = e^{iT^*t}\Delta(x)$
$\Delta(\varphi_t(x)) = e^{iT^*t}\Delta(x)$ in the complexified representation, with period $T^* = 2\pi$.
$\Delta(\varphi_t(x)) = e^{iT^*t}\Delta(x)$ na representação complexificada, $T^* = 2\pi$.
Δ(φ_t(x)) = e^{iT*t} Δ(x), T* = 2π

This axiom ties Axiom 3 (the temporal field $T$ with Reeb flow $\varphi_t$) to the dimensional field. Following the Reeb orbit for time $t$ rotates the entire phase vector by the complex phase $e^{iT^*t}$. After one full period $T^* = 2\pi$, the phase vector returns to itself: $e^{i \cdot 2\pi} = 1$. This is why $T^* = 2\pi$ is not a convention but a consequence — it is the period that makes the field single-valued on the closed Reeb orbit.

Definitions 2.1–2.4
Definition 2.1 — Phase Subspace
$D_i = \{x \in M : \delta_i(x) = \max_j \delta_j(x)\}$ — the region where the $i$-th phase component dominates.
Definition 2.2 — Phase Projection
$\pi_i : M \to \mathbb{R}$ given by $\pi_i(x) = \delta_i(x)$. The phases partition the action of $\Delta$ into twelve observable components.
Definition 2.3 — Perpendicular Field
$\Delta_\perp(O_i(x)) = A_i v - \Pi(A_i v)$ where $v = \Delta(x)$ and $\Pi(w) = \langle w, v\rangle / \|v\|^2 \cdot v$ is the orthogonal projection onto $\mathrm{span}\{v\}$. This is the component of the operator output that is perpendicular to the current phase vector — the quantity that the Orthogonality Theorem (Ch 5) controls.
Definition 2.4 — The Twelve Operators (preview)
$O_i$ maps $D_i$ into $D_{i+1 \bmod 12}$ with matrix action $\Delta(O_i(x)) = A_i \Delta(x)$, where $A_i = P^i + u_i w_i^T$ (permutation matrix plus rank-1 correction). Formally defined in Chapter 3.
The 12-Phase Structure

Each of the four base operators ($C$, $K$, $F$, $U$) contributes three dimensional operators — one per invariant ($I_1$ orthogonality, $I_2$ nilpotency, $I_3$ spectral collapse). $4 \times 3 = 12$ phases:

D₁Compress–OrthC · I₁
D₂Compress–NilpC · I₂
D₃Compress–SpecC · I₃
D₄Curvature–OrthK · I₁
D₅Curvature–NilpK · I₂
D₆Curvature–SpecK · I₃
D₇Fold–OrthF · I₁
D₈Fold–NilpF · I₂
D₉Fold–SpecF · I₃
D₁₀Unfold–OrthU · I₁
D₁₁Unfold–NilpU · I₂
D₁₂Unfold–SpecU · I₃

The cyclic structure $D_{i+12} \equiv D_i$ (Axiom 5) means the phase index is always taken modulo 12. The dimensional field $\Delta$ assigns to every point in $M$ a unit-norm vector in $\mathbb{R}^{12}$ that records which phase is currently dominant and how much energy each phase carries.
