Step 1 · Passo 1 · Sit · Sentar
English

Sit down in Lotus or Half Lotus. If neither is comfortable today, sit with your spine upright and your hands resting open on your knees. The posture is not the practice — the posture is the container for the practice. Find the position where your body can be still and your attention can move freely.

Português

Sentem-se em Lotus ou Meio Lotus. Se nenhuma das posições for confortável hoje, sente-se com a coluna ereta e as mãos abertas nos joelhos. A postura não é a prática — a postura é o recipiente da prática. Encontrem a posição onde o corpo pode ficar quieto e a atenção pode mover-se livremente.

Lotus e Meio Lotus — guia de postura · wildmind.org (PT) Step 2 · Passo 2 · Learn the Guru Puja Mantras · Aprenda os Mantras do Guru Puja
English

Learn the Guru Puja Mantras. The mantras are not prayers asking for something from outside. They are recognitions — statements of what is already true about the nature of the guru, the lineage, and your relationship to both. You chant them to align your awareness with what they name.

Português

Aprenda os Mantras do Guru Puja. Os mantras não são orações pedindo algo de fora. São reconhecimentos — declarações do que já é verdadeiro sobre a natureza do guru, da linhagem, e da sua relação com ambos. Você os canta para alinhar sua consciência com o que eles nomeiam.

🌹 🌹 🌹

Roses at the feet of the guru. Take flowers in your hands. Hold them. At the end of each mantra, offer them — place them at the image of Sri Brodananda before you. What you offer with your hands your whole being offers.

English

Offering flowers is the external gesture of an internal offering. The rose is the mind made beautiful and surrendered. The act of placing it at the guru's feet is the act of placing your attention — not scattered across the day's concerns, but gathered, directed, offered.

Português

Oferecer flores é o gesto externo de uma oferta interna. A rosa é a mente tornada bela e rendida. O ato de colocá-la aos pés do guru é o ato de colocar a atenção — não dispersa pelas preocupações do dia, mas reunida, direcionada, oferecida.

What a 3-D shape already knows · O que uma forma 3D já sabe

In the Dead Sea, salt crystallizes spontaneously into perfect cubes. No architect. No intention. Just mineral concentration crossing a threshold — and the mathematics of three mutually orthogonal dimensions selecting for the only stable form they allow at that density.

length · height · depth → g⁶ = 33

Three dimensions, all at right angles. This is what "Principia Orthogona" means at its most physical: the cube is the fixed point of orthogonal geometry. At the sixth generative level, the crystal law saturates. The Śrī Yantra's sixth āvaraṇa. The D₆ fixed point. The same structure, seen from different distances.

This image was shown to ESL students as "what is a 3-D shape?" It was already the series' central theorem — before the Lean file was written, before the operator chain had a name, before any of it. The cube knew.

But the cube only shows you three · O cubo apenas mostra três

Three dimensions — length, height, depth — at right angles. The cube saturates them. But the cube is not the end of the story. It is the beginning of the question.

Paramahamsa Nithyananda teaches, rooted in the Mrigendra Āgama and the Śiva Sūtras, that the universe runs on 11 dimensions. Five are fundamental:

1 · Length Logic, intellect, linear measurement — the axis of reason.
2 · Breadth Creativity, visualization, emotion, art, imagination — the axis of expression.
3 · Depth Oneness, Advaita, entanglement with the universe — the axis of being.
4 · Time Temporal flow, causality — and kāla as its dissolution.
5 · Space (Ākāśa) The canvas of manifestation — where thought becomes form.

The remaining six arise from combinations of these five, giving 11 total. Modern physics arrives at the same number from the opposite direction: string theory requires 10 dimensions; M-theory requires 11. The Āgamas got there first — not by measurement, but by direct perception.

The cube the ESL students held was already a teaching about all 11. Three spatial dimensions they could touch. Eight more waiting behind the surface — accessible not through a larger ruler but through a different quality of attention.

O cubo que os alunos de ESL seguravam já era um ensinamento sobre todas as 11. Três dimensões espaciais que podiam tocar. Oito mais esperando atrás da superfície — acessíveis não com uma régua maior, mas com uma qualidade de atenção diferente.
