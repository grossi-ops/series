What This Is

Beyond this point is the labyrinth — the hidden navigation layer of the Principia Orthogona. It contains chapters and machines not linked from the main sequence. Some are complete. Some are in progress. Some are conjectures held open by intention, not by failure.

This is an active research program in mathematical physics, formal verification, and sound medicine. It is a living document. You are not reading a finished book. You are reading a book that is being written in real time, in public, by a researcher who is also building the clinic that will use it.

What Is Expected of You

A student sits with difficulty. You are not permitted to conclude that something is wrong until you have returned to it three times across three different states of mind.
Distinguish between I do not understand this and this is incorrect. These are different statements. The labyrinth will test your ability to tell them apart.
Some proofs in the AXLE repository are marked sorry in Lean 4. This is not an admission of defeat. It is a precise statement about what has been verified and what remains open. A sorry is a door, not a wall.
You are not required to agree with what you find here. You are required to engage with it honestly before you form a judgment.

What This Is Not

This is not a standard academic repository. It does not seek peer review as its primary form of validation. It seeks the kind of validation that comes from correct predictions, working machines, and a patient that improves. The mathematics is accountable to biology, and biology is accountable to the patient. This is the chain.
